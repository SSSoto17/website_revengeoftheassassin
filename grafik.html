Graphics

I wanted to work with a blend of the Japanese kawaii and anime style, with a pastel colour scheme that would make the game visually appealing and soft. On this page I will take you through my design process and sources of influence.

Main Character

My main character, the assassin, was designed after kikki-principles so he would look cool and intimidating, while I made the targets bouba-shaped chibis. This design style made them more compact on the screen for gameplay purposes, and would give the effect of being vulnerable in comparison to the sharp-looking assassin.

Much inspiration was drawn from the art direction of Captain Laserhawk: A Blood Dragon Remix, the Netflix animated show from Bobbypills Studio.

The art of Studio Mir has also been an influence.

Target elements

I designed the target elements with soft and baby-like features. On the right can be seen artwork from Hyanna Natsu and Gimi Totori I took reference and inspiration from.

UI & Background

I intentionally made the shapes of the background without strokes, so only the contrast of color and soft gradients would define the shapes. The targets and the assassin would be drawn with lineart, so they could stand out against the background. Likewise I wanted my UI elements to carry the same softness.

I was especially influenced by the art direction on the video game Journey from Thatgamecompany.

Screens

Putting it all together, I did mockups of the gameplay, as well as the various game screens that could appear throughout the game. At this stage I was ready to begin finalising my sketches in Adobe Illustrator and put graphics into my game.
